About Our Mutual Fund Calculator

A mutual fund is a professionally managed investment fund that pools money from many investors to purchase a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities. Our Mutual Fund Calculator helps you estimate the future value of your mutual fund investments, whether you're making a one-time lumpsum investment or regular SIP contributions.

How to Use the Mutual Fund Calculator

  1. Enter Your Investment Amount: This can be a lumpsum amount or your regular SIP contribution.
  2. Enter the Expected Annual Return: This is the anticipated annual growth rate of your mutual fund investment.
  3. Enter the Time Period: This is the duration in years for which you plan to stay invested.
  4. Click "Calculate MF": The calculator will provide an estimate of the future value of your investment, including the total invested amount and the potential returns.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a mutual fund?
A mutual fund is a type of financial vehicle made up of a pool of money collected from many investors to invest in securities like stocks, bonds, money market instruments, and other assets.
What's the difference between a SIP and a lumpsum investment?
A SIP involves investing a fixed amount regularly over a period, while a lumpsum investment is a one-time investment of a large sum.
Are the returns from a mutual fund guaranteed?
No, mutual fund investments are subject to market risks. The returns are not guaranteed and will depend on the performance of the underlying assets in the fund.
